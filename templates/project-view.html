User Research

Insights were gathered from users based on their experiences with online payments, subscription services, and existing virtual or physical card solutions. This helped uncover common frustrations around card declines, spending limits, and unclear transaction flows.

Pain Points

  1. Frequent card declines when paying for international services or subscriptions.
  2. Limited control over spending and card limits across different use cases.
  3. Confusion around card activation, funding, and transaction status.
  4. Concerns about security when entering card details online.
  5. Difficulty managing multiple cards for different purposes.

Research And Design Process

Why This Process Worked

This structured approach ensured that design decisions were rooted in real user problems while balancing business needs, security requirements, and scalability. The result was a seamless virtual card experience that feels intuitive, trustworthy, and easy to use.

Research

  • Reviewed existing virtual card products and feedback
  • Identified common online payment and card usage challenges

Analysis

  • Synthesized findings into key pain points and opportunities
  • Defined core problems around clarity and reliability

Design

  • Created flows for onboarding, creation, funding, transactions
  • Designed clear status and feedback states

Test

  • Tested flows to identify usability and friction points
  • Iterated designs based on feedback and observations
