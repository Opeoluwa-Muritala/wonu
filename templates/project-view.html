Smarter virtual cards for online payments

Role UI/UX Design
Year Feb 2025 – August 2025

AV Card Platform

Overview

Avcard is a virtual card product that allows users to instantly create, fund, and manage digital cards for online payments, subscriptions, and global transactions. The platform focuses on speed, security, and user control—eliminating common issues like card limits and payment failures.

What Wasn’t Working?

  1. Frequent Card Declines for Online Payments

    Users experienced failed transactions when paying for international services, subscriptions, and online purchases—leading to frustration and loss of trust.

  2. Limited Control Over Spending

    Fixed card limits, unclear balances, and poor transaction visibility made spending management difficult.

  3. Complex Card Setup and Activation

    Existing solutions required too many steps before users could create, fund, and use cards.

  4. Low Trust in Card Security

    Unclear PIN flows and limited transparency caused hesitation around online card usage.

How We Solved It

Avcard was designed to give users full control over their online payments through fast virtual card creation, flexible spending controls, and secure transaction flows. By simplifying onboarding, improving transparency, and prioritizing security, the product removes common payment pain points.

Key Solutions Implemented

  1. Instant Virtual Card Creation

    Users can create new virtual cards in seconds without complex verification steps.

  2. Custom Card Controls

    Each card can be named, funded individually, and assigned spending limits.

  3. Clear Activation & Status Feedback

    Users are clearly informed when a card is created, inactive, activated, or funded.

  4. Secure PIN-Based Transactions

    Every transaction requires a 4-digit PIN, increasing user confidence.

  5. Tier-Based Access with Clear Value

    Account tiers are clearly communicated with features available at each level.

  6. Transparent Transaction History

    Users can view transaction details in a clean, organized layout.

Target Users

Freelancers, remote workers, online shoppers, and small business owners who frequently make online and international payments and need a reliable, flexible alternative to traditional bank cards.

User Research

Insights were gathered from users based on their experiences with online payments, subscription services, and existing virtual or physical card solutions. This helped uncover common frustrations around card declines, spending limits, and unclear transaction flows.

Pain Points

  • Frequent card declines when paying for international services or subscriptions.
  • Limited control over spending and card limits across different use cases.
  • Confusion around card activation, funding, and transaction status.
  • Concerns about security when entering card details online.
  • Difficulty managing multiple cards for different purposes.

Research And Design Process

Why This Process Worked

This structured approach ensured that design decisions were rooted in real user problems while balancing business needs, security requirements, and scalability. The result was a seamless virtual card experience that feels intuitive, trustworthy, and easy to use.

Research

  • Reviewed existing virtual card products and feedback
  • Identified common online payment and card usage challenges

Analysis

  • Synthesized findings into key pain points and opportunities
  • Defined core problems around clarity and reliability

Design

  • Created flows for onboarding, creation, funding, transactions
  • Designed clear status and feedback states

Test

  • Tested flows to identify usability and friction points
  • Iterated designs based on feedback and observations
