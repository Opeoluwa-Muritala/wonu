Overview

Avcard is a virtual card product that allows users to instantly create, fund, and manage digital cards for online payments, subscriptions, and global transactions. The platform focuses on speed, security, and user control, eliminating common issues like card limits and payment failures.

What Wasn't Working?

1. Frequent Card Declines for Online Payments

Users often experience failed transactions when trying to pay for international services, subscriptions, or online purchases. This creates frustration and a lack of trust in digital payment platforms.

2. Limited Control Over Spending

Most users struggle to manage how much they spend online due to fixed card limits, unclear balances, and poor transaction visibility.

3. Complex Card Setup and Activation Flows

Existing virtual card solutions often require too many steps before a user can successfully create, fund, and use a card, leading to drop-offs during onboarding.

4. Low Trust in Card Security

Users are cautious about online payments due to fraud risks, unclear PIN flows, and limited transparency around how their card details are protected.

How We Solved It

Avcard was designed to give users full control over their online payments through fast virtual card creation, flexible spending controls, and secure transaction flows. By simplifying onboarding, improving transparency, and prioritizing security, the product removes common pain points associated with online payments and card failures.

2. Custom Card Controls

Each card can be named, funded individually, and assigned spending limits, allowing users to manage subscriptions, one-time purchases, and business expenses with ease.

Research And Design Process

Why This Process Worked

This structured approach ensured that design decisions were rooted in real user problems while balancing business needs, security requirements, and scalability. The result was a seamless virtual card experience that feels intuitive, trustworthy, and easy to use.

Research
  • Reviewed existing virtual card products and user feedback
  • Identified common online payment and card usage challenges
Analysis
  • Synthesized findings into key pain points and opportunities
  • Defined core problems around card reliability, control, and clarity
Design
  • Created user flows for onboarding, card creation, funding, and transactions
  • Designed intuitive interfaces with clear status and feedback states
Test
  • Tested flows to identify usability issues and friction points
  • Iterated designs based on feedback and observations
